The moment when you realize you validated the product. -- week 14

13 April 2015 on weekly-update, lean-startup

This week in the world of me2.

This week I began to work on building the product! I have not been able to dig into the code quite yet (the suspense is killing me) but I began to at least start the process of developing the product.

Stack of my user stories created this week.

I spent the early part of the week making phone calls as well as reflecting on past notes I wrote down during interviews. I have had some parts of the me2 product that I have been on the edge about for a while and I needed some clarification.

After hanging up these calls and reading of past notes, I put on my head phones, turned on a long mix of music, grabbed a couple notepads and wrote as many user stories as I could possibly think of. Time to start connecting the dots!

I...I...I think the product is validated.

Looking at the product right now compared to where it was back in October at Startup Weekend is absolutely amazing. Back then, I thought the product was pretty solid and was ready to continue developing it immediately after the weekend was over. After starting on it full-time, reading about the lean-startup methodology and going through Venture School I really started to think, do I really know that this product is solid?

I have been asking potential users questions for months now trying to get a better idea of how this product should be developed. Every single step along the way there were times where I thought, "This thing is ready to build. I can see just what the users are looking for in this product." Then two minutes later I thought, "Awwww, I am still on the edge about this part. What am I going to do about this situation? What if...". More validation had to be done on these parts.

After I got off the phone early this week I had a moment. I had this feeling inside telling me that this is what the users are looking for. They helped answer those last few big questions on my mind and I finally had this feeling where I knew what my users were looking for in a product. My product validation hit a sweet point where I actually knew what the users were looking for! Wow, I have never had a feeling like that before!

When you feel insecure, ask more questions.

For the past couple of months there have been parts of the product that I was not very comfortable with. Parts that I wasn't quite sure if the users would find it valuable and parts that I felt were going to be really difficult to overcome (I felt these parts could be overcome but also felt there had to be a better way). I reflected on these insecurities and wondered if those were things that I was just going to have to deal with or if there was a way to overcome these challenges better. I decided to just keep doing more validation.

Then it was this week after I got off the phone that I had the big "Aha!" moment. All of those insecurities seemed to float away. I have no idea how it happened, but while I was on the phone I asked a couple questions that got my friends going off on the path I was looking for. They gave me information that I have been searching for months for.

I guess patience is a good thing sometimes. You will know when the product has been validated. You will get a gut feeling inside telling you that you hit a sweet spot. Just keep asking questions until you get that feeling.

Having a validated product feels oh, so good!

Product validation has taken some time, but reflecting on what the product has turned into and how confident I am about the product now I would say it is very much worth it. I have been starting businesses half my life and programming just as long and I have never been this confident in what the customers/users were looking for in the product. It feels oh, so good! Now it is time to go start building!

Everyone struggles at some point in life. We are here to say #me2. You are not alone. @me2nation
